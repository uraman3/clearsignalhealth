The Problem

AI is reshaping cancer
care. Most patients don't know it.

AI-assisted tools for breast cancer screening: mammography analysis, risk stratification models, imaging interpretation are being deployed across major health systems at an accelerating pace. Yet the communities these tools are meant to serve remain largely uninformed: about how these systems work, what their limitations are, and how to advocate for equitable, accurate care.

Women of color, underinsured women, and those outside major research centers face documented disparities in both breast cancer outcomes and AI model accuracy. The bottleneck is not the technology — it is literacy, access, and accountability.

Our Approach

Four ways we close the gap

ClearSignal Health delivers community-rooted programs that move women from passive recipients of AI-assisted care to informed, empowered advocates.

01

AI Health Literacy Workshops

Community sessions that demystify AI in breast cancer screening — what it detects, where it falls short, and how to ask better questions of your care team.

02

ClearSignal Resource Hub

Plain-language digital resources translating current AI breast cancer research for patients, caregivers, and community health workers without the jargon.

03

Policy Literacy Sessions

Education for community leaders and advocates on the regulatory landscape governing AI medical tools — who is accountable, and how to engage the systems that shape care.

04

Train-the-Trainer Basecamps

Scalable programs embedding health AI literacy in existing community organizations, clinics, and advocacy groups across the Pacific Northwest.
